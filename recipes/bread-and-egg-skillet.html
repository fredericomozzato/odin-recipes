Bread and Egg Skillet

This recipe was found in the Spain on a Fork website. This is a great collection of spanish recipes and I highly recommend the Youtube channel for very instructive and well made videos. You can find this recipe's video here.

According to the original recipe this is a dish that originates from the town of Juncar in Spain. It is ready in just 30 minutes and use simple and tasty ingredients.

    Ingredients
  • 3 table spoons of extra virgin olive oil (45 ml)
  • 4 slices of baguette (around 2.g cm thick each)
  • 1 onion
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 green bell peppers
  • 4 tomatoes
  • 1/2 table spoon of sweet smoked paprika (1.15 g)
  • 1/2 table spoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth
  • 4 eggs
  • a pinch of sea salt
  • black pepper
  • white sugar
  • fresh parsley

    Instructions:
  1. Cut each slice of baguette into 6 evenly sized cubes. Roughly chop the onion, thinly slice the garlic, cut the bell peppers into small bite-sized pieces and finely grate the tomatoes
  2. Heat a large fry pan with a medium heat and the olive oil for a couple of minutes
  3. Add the cubes of bread to the pan, mix continuously. After 3 or 4 minutes, when they're golden fried, remove them from the pan and set them aside
  4. With the same pan and the same heat add in the onion, bell pepper and garlic. Mix with the olive oil for about 5 minutes. When the vegetables are lightly sauteed add in the paprika and thyme, quickly mix it together, then add the grated tomatoes and season it with sea salt, black pepper and a little bit of white sugar. Mix it together and let it simmer
  5. Once the grated tomato has slightly thickened up (around 5 minutes of simmering) add in the pieces of fried bread back into the pan, along with the vegetable broth. Give it a mix and bring it to a boil. Then add in the eggs, making sure to evenly spread them around. Then place lid on the pan and lower to a low-medium heat. Cook until the eggs are just cooked through (between 3 to 5 minutes)
  6. Serve it directly out of the pan, sprinkled with finelly chopped parsley
  7. Enjoy!

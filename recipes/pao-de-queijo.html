Pão de queijo (brazillian cheese-bread)

Pão de queijo, or in a free translation, cheese-bread, is a traditional brazillian recipe originating from the state of Minas Gerais. It is a dough consisting of cassava starch, eggs and cheese that is rolled in little balls and baked.

    Ingredients
  • 150 ml of oil (a neutral oil is recommended, like cottonseed or sunflower oil)
  • 150 ml of water
  • 150 ml of milk
  • 500g of sour cassava starch
  • 13g of sea salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 200g of grated semi-cured cheese
  • 25g of grated parmesan cheese

    Instructions
  1. In a pan add the water, milk, oil and let it boil
  2. Place the sour starch and the salt in a large bowl
  3. When the liquid reaches the boiling stage pour it over the starch and mix it well with a wooden spoon. Then let the mixture cool down a bit
  4. Add the eggs and mix it with the hands to incorporate the ingredients well
  5. Add both cheeses and mix it well with the hands
  6. Clean your hands from the mixtures residues before modelling the breads. If the dough is too sticky spread a little oil in your hands. Roll a little bit of dough with your hands to form a ball
  7. Pre-heat the oven at 180oC for around 20 minutes
  8. Place the breads in a baking tray and bake it for around 35 minutes

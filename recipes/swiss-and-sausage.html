Sausage and swiss cheese

For this sandwich you'll use a mild ground sausage. In my recipe I use a specific variety called Blumenau Sausage, that is used as a spread on bread. The bread we use is the ciabatta, and the cheese is Gruyère.

    Ingredients
  • Ciabatta
  • Mild ground sausage
  • Sliced Gruyère cheese
  • Red onion
  • Olive oil
  • Mustard

    Instructions
  1. Slice the ciabatta in half and put a little bit of olive oil in it. Toast the bread in a pan with low heat until golden
  2. Get some of the sausage and spread it in a plate, mold it in the form of the bread, pressing gently to form a sort of sausage patty
  3. Carefully remove the patty from the plate with the help of a spatula and place it in the pan. If you use a non adherent there's no need for any oil
  4. Place a slice of Gruyère cheese on top of the sausage, place a lid on top of it and wait for the cheese to melt
  5. Spread a little bit of mustard on the ciabatta, place the sliced onion on the bottom slice
  6. When the cheese melts carefully remove the sausage from the pan and place it on the bread
  7. You can also add some mayo on the upper slice of bread, then close the sandwich and enjoy it!
